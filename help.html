Using the AI Analysis

Preparing Your Images

📱

Camera Quality

Use a high-resolution camera (smartphone cameras work well). Ensure the lens is clean and scratch-free.

💡

Lighting

Natural daylight is best. Avoid direct flash, harsh shadows, or yellow indoor lighting.

🎯

Focus & Framing

Fill 50-75% of the frame with the lesion. Keep the camera steady and ensure sharp focus.

📏

Size Reference

Include a coin, ruler, or your finger for size comparison. This helps our AI assess the lesion's dimensions.

Understanding Results

Result Categories

Confidence Score: 70-95% typically indicates a benign lesion.

What it means: The AI believes this lesion is likely non-cancerous.

Next steps: Continue regular self-examinations and routine dermatologist visits.

Remember: Even benign lesions should be monitored for changes over time.

Confidence Score: 40-70% indicates uncertainty in classification.

What it means: The AI cannot make a confident determination.

Next steps: Consult with a dermatologist for professional evaluation.

Common causes: Poor image quality, unusual lesion characteristics, or lighting issues.

Confidence Score: 70%+ for potentially malignant characteristics.

What it means: The lesion shows features that warrant immediate medical attention.

Next steps: Schedule an appointment with a dermatologist as soon as possible.

Important: Early detection and treatment significantly improve outcomes.

ABCDE Rule Reference

🅰️

Asymmetry

One half of the mole doesn't match the other half in shape, color, or thickness.

🅱️

Border

Edges are irregular, ragged, notched, or blurred rather than smooth and even.

🎨

Color

Multiple colors within the same lesion or color that has changed over time.

📏

Diameter

Larger than 6mm (about the size of a pencil eraser) or growing in size.

📈

Evolving

Changes in size, shape, color, elevation, or symptoms like bleeding or itching.

💡 Remember: Our AI considers all these factors, but a dermatologist's trained eye is irreplaceable for definitive diagnosis.

Troubleshooting

Common Issues

Solutions:

  • Check your internet connection
  • Ensure image is under 10MB and in PNG, JPG, or JPEG format
  • Try compressing the image or using a different file
  • Clear your browser cache and try again
  • Try using a different browser or device

Common causes and solutions:

  • Poor lighting: Retake in natural daylight
  • Blurry image: Ensure camera is steady and focused
  • Too far/close: Lesion should fill 50-75% of frame
  • Shadows: Avoid direct flash, use diffused lighting
  • Hair covering lesion: Gently part hair for clear view

Try these steps:

  • Refresh the page and try again
  • Use a different image of the same lesion
  • Check if the image actually contains a skin lesion
  • Ensure stable internet connection
  • Contact support if problems persist

Account access issues:

  • Verify your email address is correct
  • Check spam folder for verification emails
  • Try password reset if you forgot your password
  • Clear browser cookies and cache
  • Contact support for account recovery assistance

Safety Guidelines

🚨 Critical Safety Information

SkinGuard AI is a screening tool, NOT a diagnostic tool. Never delay seeking professional medical care based on our AI results.

When to See a Doctor Immediately

🔴

Concerning Results

Any result flagged as concerning or potentially malignant requires immediate professional evaluation.

📈

Rapid Changes

Lesions that change quickly in size, color, or texture need immediate medical attention.

🩸

Bleeding or Pain

Any lesion that bleeds, becomes painful, or develops unusual symptoms requires prompt evaluation.

❓

When in Doubt

If you're concerned about any skin lesion, regardless of AI results, consult a healthcare professional.

Regular Monitoring

📅 Recommended Schedule:
  • Self-examinations: Monthly full-body skin checks
  • Professional exams: Annual dermatologist visits
  • High-risk individuals: More frequent professional monitoring
  • Document changes: Use our platform to track lesions over time

Limitations of AI Analysis

  • Provide definitive medical diagnosis
  • Replace professional dermatological examination
  • Analyze lesions in sensitive areas (eyes, mouth, genitals)
  • Detect all types of skin cancer with 100% accuracy
  • Consider your personal/family medical history
  • Perform physical examination or biopsy
